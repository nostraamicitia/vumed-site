CASUS kwalitatief onderzoek 4 vragen

Background Intervention trials confirm that patients with advanced cancer receiving early palliative care experience a better quality of life and show improved knowledge about and use of palliative care services. To involve patients in future healthcare decisions, health professionals should understand patients’ perspectives. However, little is known about how patients experience such interventions. The aim of this study is to explore advanced cancer patients’ experiences with a structured early palliative care intervention, its acceptability and impact on the patients’

life. Methods In this qualitative study, we employed semi-structured, open-ended, face-to-face interviews. Participants were recruited via purposive sampling. The interview guide was based on guides from prior studies. We initially interviewed and preliminary analyzed data from 10 participants but continued sampling until we reached data saturation (N = 20). We followed an inductive coding. The first author performed the main analyses. The first and the second author coded and analyzed independently four interviews to later compare the coding approach and make sure that the coding was intersubjectively comprehensible. After all interviews were coded, the first and second author through repeated intensive discussions adapted and consolidated the final categories and subcategories. Gebaseerd op: Fliedner, Monica, et al. "An early palliative care intervention can be confronting but reassuring: A qualitative study on the experiences of patients with advanced cancer." Palliative medicine 33.7 (2019): 783-792.
